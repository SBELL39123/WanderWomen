ST. LOUIS Guide

Midwestern city with southern charm

Top Tourist Spots

Always time for baseball, blues, and bbq

St. Louis Arch

The St. Louis Arch, also known as the Gateway Arch, stands as a symbol of the city's role in the westward expansion of the United States. At 630 feet, it is the tallest arch in the world and offers spectacular views from the top.

Cardinal's Stadium

Busch Stadium, home of the St. Louis Cardinals, offers fans a chance to experience Major League Baseball in a beautiful downtown setting. The stadium features a state-of-the-art design and incredible views of the St. Louis skyline.

Forest Part

Forest Park is one of the largest urban parks in the U.S., offering over 1,300 acres of green space, museums, and outdoor activities. Visitors can explore the Saint Louis Art Museum, the Missouri History Museum, and more, making it a perfect family destination.

City Museum

The City Museum is a one-of-a-kind museum in St. Louis, offering interactive exhibits and a giant playground. With features like tunnels, slides, and a rooftop Ferris wheel, it's fun for all ages.

Cathedral Basilica of St. Louis

The Cathedral Basilica of St. Louis is a stunning example of Byzantine-style architecture, featuring beautiful mosaics that cover the interior. It's a peaceful place for reflection and admiration of its artwork and history.

Cahokia Mounds

Cahokia Mounds is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located near St. Louis. This ancient Native American site is home to massive earthen mounds and was once the center of a thriving civilization, offering visitors a glimpse into pre-Columbian life.

Hidden Gems

​St. Louis is home to numerous lesser-known attractions that showcase its rich history and culture.

Vintage Vinyl

Vintage Vinyl is a popular record store and local hub for music lovers in St. Louis. With an extensive collection of vinyl records and live performances, it’s a must-visit for anyone who enjoys music and local culture.

Delmar Loop

The Delmar Loop is a lively St. Louis neighborhood known for its eclectic mix of restaurants, shops, and entertainment venues. It's home to live music spots, vintage theaters, and plenty of local art, making it a cultural hotspot.

Grand Blvd

Grand Blvd is a historically rich area in St. Louis that offers a mix of local shops, restaurants, and cultural sites. It's perfect for exploring St. Louis's unique neighborhoods, offering both old-world charm and modern amenities.

Central West End

The Central West End is an upscale neighborhood in St. Louis, known for its beautiful architecture, boutique shopping, and vibrant dining scene. It's also home to the renowned Missouri Botanical Garden and Forest Park, making it a perfect spot for both relaxation and exploration.

Soulard Market

Soulard Market is one of the oldest farmers' markets in the United States, located in the Soulard neighborhood. It's a great place to find fresh produce, local meats, and unique crafts, offering a taste of St. Louis’s vibrant community culture.

Cultural Encounters

St. Louis, affectionately known as the "Gateway to the West," boasts a rich tapestry of cultural influences that have shaped its unique identity. From its historic landmarks to its vibrant arts scene, the city offers a diverse array of experiences that reflect its storied past and dynamic present.

Black Culture in St. Louis

  • The Griot Museum of Black History: Dedicated to preserving and sharing the rich stories of Black history and culture, featuring life-size wax figures, historical artifacts, and immersive exhibits. Learn more
  • Old St. Louis Courthouse: The site of the Dred Scott decision, this historic courthouse has been restored and offers insights into significant events in African American history. Read more
  • The Black Rep: Founded in 1976, this theater company is committed to producing works by Black playwrights, creating opportunities for new voices, and celebrating the rich cultural heritage of the community. Discover their mission

Cherokee Street

Cherokee Street is a vibrant cultural district known for its eclectic mix of antique shops, art galleries, and diverse restaurants. The street hosts various cultural events throughout the year, reflecting the city's rich heritage.

Bosnian Culture in St. Louis

  • Bevo Mill Neighborhood: Known as "Little Bosnia," this area is a hub for Bosnian culture, offering authentic cuisine, shops, and the Sebilj Fountain, a replica of the one in Sarajevo. Explore the neighborhood
  • St. Louis Bosnians, Inc.: An organization dedicated to sharing Bosnian-American culture, language, history, and cuisine through educational programs and events. Get involved
  • International Institute of St. Louis: Since 1993, this institute has resettled over 6,500 Bosnian refugees, contributing to the city's diverse cultural tapestry. Learn more

Creole/Cajun Culture in St. Louis

St. Louis's Creole and Cajun communities contribute to the city's cultural diversity through:

  • Culinary Delights: Restaurants offering dishes like gumbo, jambalaya, and crawfish étouffée.
  • Festivals: Events such as the St. Louis Cajun Festival celebrate music, dance, and cuisine native to the Louisiana region.

Southern Charm and Midwestern Spirit

  • Historical Context: Founded in 1764 by French fur traders, St. Louis later came under Spanish control, which influenced its cultural landscape. Post the Louisiana Purchase in 1803, the city became a significant point for Southern migrants heading westward, introducing Southern customs and traditions.
  • Culinary Traditions: The city's barbecue scene reflects Southern flavors, with establishments like Expat BBQ offering traditional American barbecue infused with international tastes.
  • Music and Festivals: St. Louis hosts events such as the Festival of Nations, celebrating diverse cultures, including those with Southern roots, through music, dance, and cuisine.

St. Louis Music Legends

  • Chuck Berry: Born in 1926, Chuck Berry was a pioneering figure in rock and roll, known for hits like "Maybellene" and "Johnny B. Goode." His innovative guitar riffs and energetic performances laid the foundation for future rock musicians.
  • Miles Davis: Born in 1926, Miles Davis was a legendary jazz musician who pushed the boundaries of jazz with albums like "Kind of Blue." He spent his formative years in East St. Louis, influencing the city's jazz scene.
  • Tina Turner: Moving to St. Louis at age 16, Tina Turner became a global icon with hits like "What's Love Got to Do with It." Her dynamic voice and stage presence earned her the title "Queen of Rock and Roll."
  • Grace Bumbry: Born in 1937, Grace Bumbry is a renowned mezzo-soprano opera singer who gained international acclaim for her powerful voice and performances in major opera houses worldwide.
  • Fontella Bass: Known for her hit "Rescue Me," Fontella Bass was a soul and gospel singer whose powerful vocals left a lasting impact on the music industry.

Gastronomic Delights

St. Louis cuisine is a flavorful blend of comfort food staples, featuring iconic dishes like toasted ravioli, gooey butter cake, and the savory St. Louis-style barbecue. *All can be veganized or vegetarianized.*

To Eat

  • St. Louis BBQ: A culinary tradition in St. Louis, this barbecue style features spare ribs that are typically grilled, resulting in a flavorful and tender dish. Renowned establishments like Pappy's Smokehouse and Salt + Smoke BBQ are celebrated for their exceptional barbecue offerings.
  • Toasted Ravioli: A beloved appetizer in St. Louis, toasted ravioli consists of breaded and fried pasta filled with seasoned beef, often served with marinara dipping sauce. This dish is a staple in many local Italian restaurants.
  • Gooey Butter Cake: A sweet dessert originating from St. Louis, gooey butter cake features a rich, buttery crust topped with a creamy, custard-like filling. It's a must-try treat for visitors and locals alike.
  • Frozen Custard Concrete: A popular frozen dessert in St. Louis, concretes are made by blending frozen custard with various mix-ins, creating a thick and indulgent treat. Local favorite Ted Drewes serves this delightful concoction.
  • St. Paul Sandwiches: A unique St. Louis creation, the St. Paul sandwich features a breaded and fried egg foo young patty served between two slices of white bread, typically garnished with lettuce, tomato, and mayonnaise.
  • St. Louis Style Pizza: A distinctive regional pizza, St. Louis-style pizza features a thin, cracker-like crust made without yeast, topped with sweet tomato sauce, and cut into square pieces. It's traditionally topped with Provel cheese, a processed blend of cheddar, Swiss, and provolone.
  • Gus' Pretzels: Bring home the taste of St. Louis with Gus' Pretzels. These hand-twisted pretzels are a local favorite and make for a delicious souvenir.

Where to Stay

St. Louis is renowned for its diverse neighborhoods, each offering unique experiences for visitors.

Central West End

Renowned for its historic mansions, the Central West End offers a mix of antique stores, restaurants, and cafes. It's also home to the Cathedral Basilica of Saint Louis and the World Chess Hall of Fame.

Downtown St. Louis

Staying downtown provides proximity to major attractions like the Gateway Arch, City Museum, and Busch Stadium. The area also offers a variety of dining and entertainment options.

Forest Park

Larger than New York's Central Park, Forest Park features museums, walking trails, and the Saint Louis Zoo, making it ideal for families and nature enthusiasts.

The Hill

Known for its Italian-American heritage, The Hill offers authentic cuisine and a variety of restaurants, making it a food lover's destination.

Lafayette Square

Featuring historic Victorian homes and a central park, Lafayette Square provides a charming and picturesque setting for visitors.

Souvenir Ideas

Things to remember St. Louis by:

Gateway Arch Replica

Bring home a miniature version of the iconic Gateway Arch. These replicas are available in various sizes and materials, serving as a lasting reminder of your visit.

Chicago Sports Team Merchandise

Chicago is home to several beloved sports teams. Official merchandise such as jerseys, hats, and other memorabilia from the Chicago Bears (NFL), Chicago Bulls (NBA), Chicago Cubs (MLB), and Chicago Blackhawks (NHL) make for great souvenirs.

St. Louis Souvenir Collectibles

Explore various souvenir shops for items like postcards, shot glasses, keychains, mugs, and snow globes featuring St. Louis landmarks and symbols.

Provel Cheese

A St. Louis culinary staple, Provel cheese is known for its unique blend of cheddar, Swiss, and provolone. Purchase some to recreate local dishes like St. Louis-style pizza at home.

Staying Safe

To do:

1: Have Fun

2: Stay Safe

Stay Aware of Your Surroundings

Remain vigilant and attentive to your environment. Avoid distractions such as excessive phone use or wearing headphones, especially in crowded or unfamiliar areas.

Keep Personal Belongings Secure

Keep wallets, phones, and other valuables in secure, concealed locations. Consider using anti-theft bags or money belts.

Avoid High-Risk Areas

Be cautious in neighborhoods known for higher crime rates, especially after dark. It's advisable to research and identify areas to avoid before your visit.

Report Suspicious Activity

If you observe suspicious behavior or feel unsafe, contact local authorities immediately. Trust your instincts and seek assistance when needed.

Stay Informed and Prepared

  • Know Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency contacts, including local authorities and your country's embassy or consulate.
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a situation or area feels uncomfortable, trust your instincts and remove yourself from it. Prioritize your safety at all times.
