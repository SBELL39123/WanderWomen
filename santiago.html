Top Tourist Spots

​Santiago de Chile seamlessly blends colonial heritage with modern innovation, offering a rich tapestry of cultural and historical experiences.

Cerro Santa Lucia

A historic hill in the heart of the city, Cerro Santa Lucía offers panoramic views of Santiago. Its lush gardens and the Hidalgo Castle add to its charm.

Barrio Lastarria

Known for its bohemian atmosphere, Barrio Lastarria is filled with art galleries, theaters, and cozy cafes. It's a cultural hub that captures the essence of Santiago's creative spirit.

Museum of Memory and Human Rights

This museum is dedicated to preserving the memory of human rights violations during Chile's military dictatorship, offering visitors a profound and educational experience.

Metropolitan Cathedral

Located in Plaza de Armas, the Metropolitan Cathedral showcases stunning Baroque architecture and is a testament to Santiago's colonial past.

Cerro San Cristobal

Offering breathtaking views of the city and the Andes Mountains, Cerro San Cristóbal is a must-visit for nature lovers. The hill features a zoo, Japanese garden, and the iconic Virgin Mary statue at its summit.

Hidden Gems

​Santiago de Chile's lesser-known cultural and artistic spots, such as Barrio Yungay, Barrio Italia, and La Chascona, offer unique insights into the city's vibrant neighborhoods beyond typical tourist paths.

Santiago Barrio Paris-London

Located in the heart of Santiago, Barrio París-Londres is a picturesque neighborhood reminiscent of Europe's Latin Quarter. Developed in the 1920s and 1930s, it features cobblestone streets and European-style architecture, offering a charming escape from the city's hustle.

Bahá'í Temple of South America

Nestled in the foothills of the Andes, the Bahá'í Temple is a stunning architectural marvel. Its nine glass veils create a luminous space for prayer and meditation, welcoming visitors from all backgrounds.

Drugstore Boulevard

Situated in the Providencia neighborhood, Drugstore Boulevard is a lively street lined with a variety of shops, cafes, and entertainment venues, offering a vibrant atmosphere for both locals and tourists.

Igelsia San Fransico

This historic church, located near Barrio París-Londres, showcases colonial architecture and is one of the oldest religious buildings in Santiago, offering a serene spot for reflection.

La Moneda Palace

The presidential palace of Chile, La Moneda, is an iconic building that stands as a symbol of the nation's history and political significance, offering guided tours that provide insight into its rich heritage.

Santa Lucia Craft Fair

Held in the picturesque Santa Lucía Hill park, this craft fair features local artisans showcasing handmade goods, providing a perfect opportunity to experience Chile's vibrant arts and crafts scene.

Mall Casa Costanera

Located in the Vitacura neighborhood, Mall Casa Costanera offers a luxurious shopping experience with a variety of high-end retailers, gourmet dining options, and panoramic city views from its rooftop.

Cultural Encounters

From its vibrant nightlife to its traditional customs, the city offers a rich cultural experience that embraces its deep-rooted traditions while pushing the boundaries of modernity.

Vibrant Nightlife in Bellavista

The Bellavista neighborhood is Santiago's premier nightlife district, offering a variety of bars, nightclubs, and live music venues that come alive after sunset.

Cueca Dance Performances

Experience Chile's national dance, the Cueca, characterized by lively footwork and colorful handkerchiefs, reflecting the country's rich cultural heritage.

Traditional Chilean Cuisine

Indulge in Santiago's culinary delights, from classic dishes like empanadas and pastel de choclo to innovative fusion cuisine, showcasing the city's gastronomic diversity.

Gastronomic Delights

​Santiago's dynamic culinary scene melds Italian, Spanish, and indigenous influences, offering everything from traditional Chilean dishes like pastel de choclo and cazuela to innovative fusion cuisine in both casual eateries and upscale restaurants. *All can be veganized or vegetarianized.*

To Eat

  • Completos: A Chilean twist on the classic hot dog, the completo is a hearty sandwich featuring a sausage nestled in a soft roll, generously topped with mashed avocado, diced tomatoes, mayonnaise, and sauerkraut. The 'Completo Italiano' variant adds tomato to represent the colors of the Italian flag.
  • Chorrillanas: Originating from Valparaíso but beloved in Santiago, chorrillana is a filling platter of crispy French fries topped with sautéed onions, tender beef slices, and fried eggs. It's a popular choice for sharing among friends, especially during late-night gatherings.
  • Sopaipillas: Fried dough pastries that are crispy on the outside and soft inside, sopaipillas are often enjoyed with pebre (a Chilean condiment) or chancaca (a sweet syrup made from raw sugar).
  • Empanadas de Pino: These savory pastries are filled with a mixture of seasoned beef, onions, olives, hard-boiled eggs, and raisins, all encased in a flaky dough. They are a staple at any Chilean gathering.

Where to Stay

​Santiago de Chile offers a diverse range of accommodations, from historic boutique hotels in the bohemian Bellavista neighborhood to luxurious modern stays in the upscale Vitacura district, ensuring travelers experience both cultural charm and contemporary comfort.

La Chimba Hostel

La Chimba Hostel is a vibrant and sociable accommodation located in the heart of Santiago. It's an excellent choice for budget travelers seeking a lively atmosphere, offering modern amenities and easy access to local attractions.

Barrio Bellavista

Renowned for its bohemian vibe, Barrio Bellavista is Santiago's nightlife hub, featuring numerous bars, restaurants, and live music venues. It's also home to cultural landmarks like La Chascona, one of Pablo Neruda's former residences.

Barrio Lastarria

Situated near cultural institutions, Barrio Lastarria offers a blend of historic charm and modern amenities. Visitors can enjoy art galleries, theaters, and a variety of dining options in this vibrant neighborhood.

Barrio Italia

Known for its bohemian atmosphere, Barrio Italia is filled with antique shops, design stores, and cozy cafes. It's an ideal spot for travelers interested in art, design, and culinary experiences.

Souvenir Ideas

Things to remember Santiago by:

Lapis Lazuli Jewelry

Lapis lazuli, a deep-blue semi-precious stone, is native to Chile and Afghanistan. In Santiago, you can find a variety of jewelry pieces, such as necklaces, earrings, and bracelets, crafted from this beautiful stone.

Mapuche Textiles

Greda refers to handmade clay pottery, traditionally used for cooking. Items such as bowls, plates, and cooking pots are crafted from this durable material, enhancing the flavors of Chilean cuisine.

Pablo Neruda Poetry Books

As the birthplace of Nobel laureates Pablo Neruda and Gabriela Mistral, Chile offers a rich literary heritage. Acquiring a collection of Neruda's poetry provides insight into the nation's soul.

Phone Safety

  • Keep Your Phone Secure: Petty theft is common in busy areas like tourist attractions and public transport. Avoid using your phone in crowded spaces and always keep it in a secure pocket or bag. Consider using a phone pouch that’s hard to access from the outside.
  • Avoid Flashy Displays of Wealth: Buenos Aires is a vibrant city, but like many large cities, it’s important to be discreet. Avoid flaunting expensive jewelry, high-end cameras, or large amounts of cash. Keep a low profile to minimize attention and reduce the risk of theft.

Avoid Demonstrations

Chile has experienced demonstrations that can disrupt transportation and pose safety risks. It's advisable to avoid demonstrations and follow the instructions of local authorities, including movement restrictions and curfews.

Be Prepared for Natural Disasters

Chile is located in a highly active seismic zone, and earthquakes are relatively common. Familiarize yourself with safety or evacuation procedures at your accommodation.

Stay Informed and Prepared

  • Know Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency contacts, including local authorities and your country's embassy or consulate.
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a situation or area feels uncomfortable, trust your instincts and remove yourself from it. Prioritize your safety at all times.
