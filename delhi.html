DELHI Guide

In India for the best Indian food

Top Tourist Spots

Delhi: Where history meets hustle.

Qutub Minar

A UNESCO World Heritage Site, this towering minaret stands at 72.5 meters and is the tallest brick minaret in the world. It's renowned for its intricate carvings and inscriptions.

Red Fort (Lal Qila)

A 17th-century fortification, the Red Fort is a symbol of India's sovereignty. Its massive red sandstone walls enclose a complex of museums and gardens.

Humayun's Tomb

This Mughal-era tomb is the first garden tomb in India and is believed to have inspired the Taj Mahal. Its symmetrical gardens and Persian-style architecture are a visual treat.

India Gate

A war memorial dedicated to soldiers who died during World War I, India Gate is an iconic landmark. The surrounding lawns are a popular spot for picnics and evening outings.

Connaught Place

A bustling commercial and financial hub in New Delhi, Connaught Place features a circular market with two concentric circles housing numerous shops, restaurants, and entertainment venues. Visitors can explore a variety of retail outlets, from international brands to local handicrafts, making it a shopper's paradise.

Taj Mahal

Located in Agra, approximately a 3-hour drive from Delhi, the Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum commissioned in 1631 by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. It's renowned for its stunning architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Hidden Gems

Explore Delhi's vibrant cultural scene in neighborhoods like Hauz Khas Village and Lodhi Colony, where historic sites, contemporary art, and unique shops narrate the city's rich and artistic heritage. These areas provide an intimate glimpse into Delhi's cultural life, away from the usual tourist paths.

Hauz Khas Village

A trendy area known for its blend of medieval history and modern urban culture. It features a 13th-century mosque, a tomb, and a water tank, all surrounded by a vibrant community of artists, designers, and boutique shops.

Lodhi Colony

Famous for its collection of 15th-century tombs, this neighborhood has transformed into an open-air art gallery. The Lodhi Art District showcases colorful murals and street art, making it a haven for art enthusiasts.

Sarojini Nagar Market

A bustling market offering a wide array of clothing, accessories, and home decor at bargain prices. It's a shopper's paradise for those seeking trendy items without breaking the bank.

Chandni Chowk

One of the oldest and busiest markets in Old Delhi, it offers a sensory overload of sights, sounds, and smells. From traditional sweets to spices and jewelry, this market is a microcosm of Delhi's rich history and diversity.

Central Park, Connaught Place

A serene green space amidst the bustling commercial district of Connaught Place. It's an ideal spot to relax, people-watch, and enjoy occasional cultural performances.

Cultural Encounters

Delhi, India's bustling capital, is a city where tradition intertwines with modernity, influencing various facets of daily life, including language, commerce, religion, attire, and transportation.

Language

Delhi's linguistic diversity is a testament to its rich history and multicultural populace. Hindi, written in the Devanagari script, serves as the official language. Urdu, with its roots in Mughal influence, is also widely spoken. English is prevalent in business, education, and governmental affairs.

Gold and Commerce

The city boasts a vibrant gold market, with areas like Chandni Chowk offering an array of gold jewelry that melds traditional craftsmanship with contemporary designs. The art of gold embroidery, known as zardozi, highlights Delhi's rich artisanal heritage.

Religion

Delhi is a microcosm of India's pluralistic society, with Hinduism, Islam, Sikhism, and Christianity practiced freely. This diversity is evident in the multitude of places of worship, including temples, mosques, gurudwaras, and churches, each providing unique insights into the city's spiritual life.

Style of Dress

Reflecting its cultural diversity and climate, Delhi's attire ranges from traditional clothing like sarees and salwar kameez for women, and kurtas or sherwanis for men, to Western attire such as jeans and shirts. Modesty is observed at religious sites, where visitors are expected to cover shoulders and knees.

Festivals

  • Holi: Celebrated in March, this festival of colors marks the arrival of spring. Participants joyfully throw colored powders, dance, and enjoy traditional sweets.
  • Diwali: The festival of lights illuminates the city with diyas (lamps), fireworks, and vibrant decorations, symbolizing the victory of light over darkness.
  • Dussehra: Marking the victory of good over evil, this festival features effigy burnings and cultural performances.

Driving Practices

Navigating Delhi's roads can be challenging due to dense traffic and unique driving customs. Lane discipline is often flexible, and honking is common. To alleviate congestion, measures like the odd-even scheme have been implemented, restricting vehicles based on license plate numbers during peak pollution periods. Visitors are advised to utilize ride-hailing services or the extensive Delhi Metro to navigate the city efficiently.

Gastronomic Delights

Delight in Delhi's rich culinary scene, as your trip concludes, you'll find yourself missing these unique tastes and Delhi's authentic flavors. *All can be veganized or vegetarianized.*

To Eat

  • Chana Masala: A spicy and tangy chickpea curry cooked in a tomato-based sauce, commonly enjoyed with rice or Indian breads like naan and roti.
  • Aloo Paratha: A popular North Indian breakfast dish consisting of a whole wheat flatbread stuffed with a spiced potato filling, served with yogurt, pickle, and sometimes butter.
  • Dal: A staple in Indian cuisine, dal refers to lentils cooked in various styles, often spiced with turmeric, cumin, and garam masala. It's commonly served with rice or Indian breads.
  • Samosas: Popular street food, samosas are triangular pastries filled with spiced potatoes, peas, or lentils, then deep-fried to a crispy golden brown. They're typically accompanied by tamarind or mint chutney.
  • Lassi: A traditional yogurt-based drink, lassi can be sweet or salty. The sweet version is often flavored with fruits like mango, while the salty variant includes spices like cumin. It's a refreshing complement to spicy meals.

Where to Stay

Explore Delhi's vibrant neighborhoods, each offering a unique blend of history, culture, and excitement.

Karol Bagh

Situated in North Delhi, Karol Bagh is renowned for its bustling markets and vibrant atmosphere. It's an excellent base for first-time visitors, offering easy access to major attractions and a wide range of accommodations.

Hauz Khas Village

Located in South Delhi, Hauz Khas Village is known for its lively nightlife, art galleries, and historic sites. The area offers a blend of modern amenities and cultural experiences, making it popular among both locals and tourists.

Connaught Place

Situated in the heart of New Delhi, Connaught Place is a major commercial and shopping hub. Its colonial-era architecture houses numerous shops, restaurants, and entertainment venues, offering a lively urban experience.

Old Delhi (Chandni Chowk)

Steeped in history, Old Delhi offers a glimpse into the city's Mughal past. The narrow lanes of Chandni Chowk are lined with shops, markets, and street food vendors, providing a sensory overload of sights, sounds, and flavors.

Souvenir Ideas

Things to remember Delhi by:

Gold Jewelry

Delhi is renowned for its exquisite gold jewelry, featuring intricate designs like Kundan and Meenakari work. These pieces are not only beautiful but also hold significant cultural value. Notable markets such as Chandni Chowk offer a wide array of options. Remember, bargaining is customary in these markets, so don't hesitate to negotiate the price.

Silk Ties

High-quality silk ties, often adorned with traditional Indian patterns and motifs, make for elegant and practical souvenirs. They blend classic style with a touch of Indian artistry. Markets like Dilli Haat are excellent places to find such items.

Spices

Delhi's markets, especially Khari Baoli, Asia's largest spice market, offer a vibrant array of spices. From aromatic cardamom to fiery red chilies, these spices can add authentic flavors to your culinary endeavors. Bargaining is common here; starting with a price about half of the initial quote is a good strategy.

Handicrafts

Artisans in Delhi produce a variety of handcrafted items, including brassware, pottery, and textiles, reflecting the city's rich artisanal traditions. Dilli Haat is a great place to explore these crafts, with artisans from different states showcasing their work.

Pashmina Shawls

Soft and luxurious, these shawls are woven from fine cashmere wool and are known for their warmth and intricate designs. Markets like Janpath and Dilli Haat offer a variety of options. Remember to negotiate the price, starting with an offer about half of the quoted price.

Wooden Carvings

Delicately carved wooden artifacts, such as boxes and figurines, showcase traditional craftsmanship and make for unique souvenirs. Explore markets like Dilli Haat and the India Craft House for a wide selection.

Staying Safe

Exercise Caution with Food and Drink

  • Choose Reputable Eateries:

    Opt for dining at well-established restaurants or those recommended by trusted sources to minimize the risk of foodborne illnesses.

  • Be Careful with Street Food:

    While street food is a significant part of Delhi's culture, ensure that the food is prepared in hygienic conditions to avoid health issues.

Be Vigilant Against Common Tourist Scams

  • Fake Hotel Closures:

    Be cautious of individuals claiming your booked hotel is closed or full, who then offer to take you to alternative accommodations. These are often scams to redirect you to overpriced or subpar lodgings. Always confirm your reservation directly with the hotel.

  • Overcharging and Fake Fees:

    Some taxi drivers or rickshaw pullers may attempt to overcharge tourists or claim additional fees. Agree on the fare beforehand or insist on using the meter. Be wary of unsolicited offers for assistance that may come with hidden costs.

  • Distraction Scams:

    Be cautious of individuals who create distractions, such as spilling something on you or asking for help, while accomplices attempt to steal your belongings. Always keep an eye on your possessions, especially in crowded areas.

Prioritize Personal Safety as a Solo Female Traveler

  • Dress Conservatively:

    Wearing modest clothing helps avoid unwanted attention and shows respect for local customs. Covering shoulders, chest, and knees is advisable, especially when visiting religious sites.

  • Stay Confident and Assertive:

    Projecting confidence can deter unwanted attention. If you encounter harassment, don't hesitate to speak out or seek help. Being assertive is important, but always prioritize your safety.

  • Use Reputable Transportation Services:

    Opt for registered taxis or ride-sharing services instead of accepting rides from unsolicited offers. Always agree on fares beforehand or insist on using the meter. Avoid traveling alone in tuk-tuks or rickshaws, as they offer no security.

  • Stay in Safe and Convenient Areas:

    Choose accommodations in well-populated, well-lit areas. South and Central Delhi are recommended for solo female travelers due to their proximity to attractions and good connectivity.

  • Be Cautious with Strangers:

    Be wary of overly friendly individuals offering unsolicited assistance, as this can be a tactic used by scammers. Trust your instincts and remove yourself from uncomfortable situations.

Blending In with Local Culture

  • Respect the Local Customs: Delhi has a rich cultural heritage. Be mindful of local traditions, such as removing your shoes when entering temples and shrines.
  • Dress Modestly: While Delhi is more relaxed than other parts of India, dressing modestly, especially when visiting religious sites, is appreciated by the locals.
  • Be Cautious with Photography: Always ask for permission before photographing people, especially in religious contexts, as some locals may find it disrespectful.

Transportation Safety

  • Use Reputable Ride-Sharing Services: When traveling by car, use reputable ride-sharing apps like Uber or 99. Avoid accepting rides from unmarked vehicles or unsolicited offers.
  • Be Cautious with Taxi Services: If using taxis, ensure they are registered and use the meter. Agree on the fare beforehand if the meter is not available.

Stay Informed and Prepared

  • Know Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency contacts, including local authorities and your country's embassy or consulate.
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a situation or area feels uncomfortable, trust your instincts and remove yourself from it. Prioritize your safety at all times.
