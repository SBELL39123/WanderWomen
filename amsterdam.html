AMSTERDAM Guide

Not your average coffeeshops

Top Tourist Spots

Amsterdam offers a blend of modern, artistic, and energetic spots perfect for young people. From trendy neighborhoods to vibrant nightlife and street art, this city is full of dynamic experiences.

Rijksmuseum

This grand museum is home to masterpieces by Rembrandt, Vermeer, and other Dutch masters. It showcases Dutch art, history, and culture in a beautiful historic building.

Anne Frank House

A poignant and emotional site, the Anne Frank House is where Anne Frank and her family hid during World War II. The museum offers a powerful glimpse into her life and the horrors of the Holocaust.

Vondelpark

Amsterdam’s largest and most popular park, Vondelpark is a green oasis perfect for a walk, bike ride, or picnic. It also features open-air concerts, theaters, and cafes.

Van Gogh Museum

Home to the world’s largest collection of Vincent van Gogh’s works, this museum offers insight into the life and artistry of one of the most influential painters in history.

Red Light District (De Wallen)

The heart of Amsterdam's nightlife and the world-renowned Red Light District is where visitors can explore its famous windows, lively bars, and diverse entertainment. While the area is known for its sex work, it's also home to an eclectic mix of restaurants, art galleries, and historic buildings.

Hidden Gems

Amsterdam is full of hidden gems, from cozy canalside cafes and secret gardens to quirky museums tucked away in charming neighborhoods, offering a unique and intimate side of the city.

Oude Kerk (Old Church)

The Oude Kerk is the oldest building in Amsterdam, dating back to the 13th century. Located in the heart of the Red Light District, it offers a peaceful and historic escape with beautiful architecture and fascinating exhibitions. It's one of the most significant landmarks in the city, blending centuries of history with contemporary art displays.

Popeye Coffeeshop

A beloved spot for both locals and visitors, Popeye Coffeeshop is known for its relaxed atmosphere and friendly service. Located near the city center, it's a perfect place to enjoy cannabis in a laid-back environment. With its central location and classic Amsterdam vibe, Popeye has become a must-visit for those looking to experience the city's famed coffeeshop culture.

Kokopelli Coffeeshop

Kokopelli is one of Amsterdam's more popular and unique coffeeshops, known for its relaxed ambiance and artistic décor. Named after the Native American spirit of music and fertility, Kokopelli attracts both cannabis enthusiasts and those seeking a chill, creative environment. The coffeeshop offers a variety of cannabis products in a cozy, vibrant space with great music and a welcoming atmosphere.

Jordaan District

A trendy and artsy neighborhood, the Jordaan is perfect for strolling through its narrow streets filled with boutiques, art galleries, and cozy cafes. The area has a relaxed vibe, and it’s one of the best places to experience local life and culture.

Art Amsterdam Spui Market

Every Sunday from March to December, Spui Square transforms into an open-air art market, where local and international artists showcase their diverse creations under the shade of plane trees.

Cultural Encounters

Amsterdam is a city where history, modern culture, and open-mindedness come together in a vibrant and welcoming atmosphere. From the famous Red Light District to its views on marijuana and magic mushrooms, to the city’s love for veganism, canals, and tulips, Amsterdam offers a unique blend of experiences for every traveler.

Red Light District Culture

The Red Light District (De Wallen) is one of Amsterdam's most famous and misunderstood areas. It’s known for its open approach to sex work, with women displaying themselves in windows, which is legal and regulated in the Netherlands. But beyond its famous windows, the district is also home to cultural venues, quirky bars, and historical landmarks like the Oude Kerk (Old Church). The area's open-minded atmosphere reflects Amsterdam's broader liberal and tolerant culture, where individuals are free to live their lives without judgment.

Marijuana and Magic Mushrooms

Amsterdam is globally recognized for its progressive stance on cannabis use. Cannabis is tolerated in "coffeeshops" around the city, where adults can buy and consume marijuana in a safe, regulated environment. The city also has a history of exploring psychedelics, including magic mushrooms, which were once sold openly before being banned. Today, "truffle" mushrooms (a legal form of psilocybin) are available in specialized shops, offering visitors a chance to experience the city's unique approach to alternative lifestyles.

Veganism in Amsterdam

Amsterdam is a paradise for vegan food lovers, with an ever-growing number of restaurants and cafes dedicated to plant-based cuisine. Whether you're looking for a casual vegan burger, artisanal plant-based cheeses, or innovative dishes like vegan sushi, Amsterdam has something to satisfy every palate. The city's commitment to sustainability and healthy living makes it a great place for vegan travelers to explore and enjoy.

Amsterdam Canals

Amsterdam’s iconic canals, with their charming bridges and historic buildings, are one of the city’s most beautiful features. The canals are perfect for leisurely boat tours or simply strolling along their banks. The UNESCO World Heritage-listed canal ring offers a picturesque setting, especially at sunset when the water reflects the vibrant lights of the city. It’s the ideal spot for relaxing, people-watching, or enjoying a peaceful moment in the heart of the city.

Tulips in Amsterdam

No trip to Amsterdam is complete without experiencing its world-famous tulips. The city comes alive with vibrant colors during the spring months, with tulip fields stretching out just outside the city. The annual Keukenhof Gardens, located nearby, is a must-see, showcasing millions of tulips in breathtaking displays. The tulip is deeply rooted in Dutch culture and history, and Amsterdam’s streets, parks, and canalsides bloom with these flowers, offering an unforgettable visual feast for visitors.

Biking Culture

Amsterdam's biking culture is legendary, with an extensive network of bike paths that stretch into the Amsterdam Metropolitan Area. Cyclists rule in Amsterdam, and great pains have been taken to accommodate them: the city is equipped with an elaborate network of cycle paths and lanes, so safe and comfortable that even toddlers and elderly people use bikes as the easiest mode of transport. This bike culture is symbolic of Amsterdam’s cultural norms, where both social and environmental sustainability are embedded into the city. Whether you're looking to rent a bike and explore the city like a local or simply observe the daily cycling routines, Amsterdam offers a unique and immersive experience for all.

Gastronomic Delights

Amsterdam's food scene is a dynamic blend of traditional Dutch flavors and innovative global influences, reflecting its rich cultural tapestry. *All can be veganized or vegetarianized.*

To Eat

  • Stamppot: A traditional Dutch dish consisting of mashed potatoes mixed with various vegetables like kale, sauerkraut, or carrots, often served with smoked sausage. It's a hearty and comforting meal, especially popular during the colder months.
  • Heineken: A popular Dutch chocolate milk drink, cherished for its rich and creamy flavor.
  • Pannenkoeken (Dutch Pancakes): Larger and thinner than American pancakes, these are often topped with ingredients like bacon, apples, or syrup. They are a beloved treat in Amsterdam.
  • Bitterballen: Deep-fried, crispy meatballs filled with a savory beef or veal ragout, commonly served as a snack with mustard during 'borreltijd' (happy hour).
  • Stroopwafels: A sweet treat consisting of two thin waffles filled with a caramel-like syrup, best enjoyed with a hot beverage.

Where to Stay

Amsterdam offers a wide range of accommodations, from historic canal-side hotels to modern design stays, ensuring a memorable visit for every traveler.

Bulldog Hostel

Located in the heart of Amsterdam's vibrant Red Light District, The Bulldog Hostel is renowned as the world's first 5-star hostel. Established in 1975, it combines a rich history with modern amenities, offering both dormitory-style rooms and private accommodations. Guests can enjoy the lively atmosphere, 24/7 on-site staff, and proximity to major attractions. The hostel also features a bar-lounge, providing a perfect spot to unwind after a day of exploration.

Jordaan Neighborhood

Situated in the heart of Amsterdam, the Jordaan is celebrated for its narrow streets, art galleries, and unique shops. Originally a working-class district, it has transformed into one of the city's most desirable areas, offering a blend of historical charm and modern amenities.

De Pijp Neighborhood

Known for its vibrant atmosphere, De Pijp is home to a diverse community and boasts a wide array of cafes, restaurants, and shops. The area is also famous for the Sarphatipark and the Albert Cuyp Market, attracting both locals and tourists alike.

Souvenir Ideas

Things to remember Amsterdam by:

Delftware Pottery

Hand-painted blue and white ceramic pieces, including tiles and figurines, reflecting Amsterdam's rich artistic heritage.

Dutch Cheese

Artisanal cheeses like Edam and Gouda, offering a flavorful slice of Dutch culinary tradition.

Wooden Clogs

Traditional handcrafted wooden footwear, often ornately decorated, symbolizing Dutch craftsmanship.

Hagelslag

Colorful sprinkles traditionally eaten on bread with butter for breakfast, a fun and sweet Dutch tradition.

Substance Safety

  • Understand Local Drug Laws: In the Netherlands, the possession, sale, or production of drugs is illegal. However, the sale of soft drugs like cannabis is tolerated in licensed 'coffeeshops' under strict conditions. Consumption outside these establishments is not permitted. [Source](https://www.government.nl/topics/drugs)
  • Be Informed About Health Policies: While taking drugs is not illegal, possession is. Always be honest about your drug consumption when seeking medical assistance, as healthcare professionals prioritize your health and will not involve the police. [Source](https://www.jellinek.nl/en/information/information-for-tourists/)

Be Vigilant Against Scams

  • Stay Alert in Crowded Areas: Be extra aware of your surroundings in busy places such as public transportation, markets, and popular tourist sites, as these are common spots for pickpockets.
  • Be Wary of Common Scams: Watch out for distractions or approaches that may be tactics used by thieves. Always keep an eye on your possessions.

Stay Informed and Prepared

  • Know Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency contacts, including local authorities and your country's embassy or consulate.
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a situation or area feels uncomfortable, trust your instincts and remove yourself from it. Prioritize your safety at all times.
