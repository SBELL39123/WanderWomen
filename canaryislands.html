CANARY ISLANDS Guide

Tranquil Tenerife and Laid-back Lanzarote

Top Tourist Spots

Experience Tenerife and Lanzarote with volcanic landscapes, unique geological formations, and prisine beaches.

Jameos del Agua

An artistic cave complex transformed by César Manrique, featuring a natural lake and unique architecture.

Mirador del Río

A viewpoint offering panoramic views of the island of La Graciosa and the surrounding ocean.

Teide National Park

Home to Spain's highest peak, Mount Teide, this park offers stunning volcanic scenery and hiking opportunities.

Santa Cruz de Tenerife

The island's capital, offering vibrant markets, cultural venues, and lively streets.

El Médano

​El Médano is a laid-back town in southern Tenerife, celebrated for its expansive golden-sand beach, Playa de El Médano, which has earned the European Blue Flag for its water quality and facilities.

Hidden Gems

The best places to soak up the sun.

Piscinas Naturales Los Abrigos

Located between the towns of Los Abrigos and La Tejita in Tenerife, these volcanic rock pools fill with seawater at high tide, offering a serene environment for swimming and relaxation.

Playa la Maretas

Situated in the municipality of Granadilla de Abona, Playa la Maretas is a tranquil beach known for its calm waters and scenic surroundings, making it ideal for families and sunbathers.

Rastro Gauzo

Visitors can explore a wide variety of stalls offering second-hand goods, antiques, clothing, and local products. The market is situated at Calle de Josefina Reverón 10, 38632, Arona, Tenerife, Spain.

Mercadillo Artesanal Las Galletas

Located in the town of Las Galletas, this artisanal market offers a variety of handmade goods, local delicacies, and souvenirs, providing visitors with a taste of Tenerife's rich cultural heritage.

Playa de Papagayo

Located in the southern part of Lanzarote, Playa de Papagayo is renowned for its crystal-clear waters and golden sands, making it a perfect spot for swimming, sunbathing, and snorkeling.

Los Volcanes Natural Park

Situated in Lanzarote, Los Volcanes Natural Park features unique volcanic landscapes, hiking trails, and diverse flora and fauna, offering visitors a chance to explore the island's geological history.

Mirador del Charco de los Clicos

Overlooking a striking green lagoon formed by volcanic activity, this viewpoint near El Golfo in Lanzarote offers breathtaking views and photographic opportunities.

Cultural Encounters

Lanzarote and Tenerife, islands of the Canary archipelago, offer a unique blend of rich cultural heritage, diverse demographics, and favorable climatic conditions, making them attractive destinations for travelers seeking both cultural enrichment and natural beauty.

Lanzarote's Cultural Landscape

Lanzarote's cultural identity is deeply influenced by the visionary artist César Manrique, whose efforts in the 1960s led to strict urban planning regulations that preserved the island's natural beauty and led to its designation as a UNESCO Biosphere Reserve in 1993.

Tenerife's Demographic Diversity

Tenerife, the largest of the Canary Islands, boasts a diverse population with a significant number of residents from various backgrounds, contributing to a rich cultural tapestry.

Favorable Climate of the Canary Islands

Both Lanzarote and Tenerife enjoy a subtropical climate, with Lanzarote experiencing mild winters and warm, sunny summers, and Tenerife offering daytime temperatures ranging between 20°C and 28°C year-round.

Gastronomic Delights

The Canary Islands offer a rich culinary experience, blending indigenous traditions with Spanish and African influences, resulting in a unique and flavorful cuisine. *All can be veganized or vegetarianized.*

To Eat

    • Papas Arrugadas con Mojo:

      Small, wrinkled potatoes boiled in seawater, served with a spicy red or green pepper sauce known as mojo. A staple of Canarian cuisine.

    • Gofio Escaldado:

      A traditional dish made by mixing gofio (toasted grain flour) with meat or fish stock, resulting in a hearty and flavorful meal.

    • Ropa Vieja Canaria:

      A savory stew combining shredded beef, chicken, potatoes, and chickpeas, seasoned with a variety of spices.

    • Sancocho Canario:

      Boiled fish served with potatoes, sweet potatoes, gofio, and mojo sauce, reflecting the islands' coastal heritage.

    • Frangollo:

      A dessert made from cornmeal, sugar, almonds, and raisins, offering a sweet end to any meal.

Where to Stay

The Canary Islands, with their stunning landscapes and diverse charm, each offer unique experiences that contribute to the overall allure of this Spanish archipelago.

Los Amigos Hostel Tenerife

Located in the heart of Tenerife, Los Amigos Hostel offers comfortable dormitories and private rooms, a vibrant terrace with stunning views of the island, and is within easy reach of local attractions like the famous Teide National Park and the beautiful beaches of Costa Adeje.

Gueldera Aprtments Lanzarote

Situated in the charming area of Lanzarote, Gueldera Apartments offer a relaxed and welcoming atmosphere. Close to the island's stunning volcanic landscapes and pristine beaches, these apartments provide a perfect base for exploring Lanzarote's unique culture, scenic trails, and vibrant towns like Puerto del Carmen.

Souvenir Ideas

Things to remember Istanbul by:

Canarian Ceramics

Vibrant, hand-painted pottery reflecting traditional motifs, perfect for adding a touch of island artistry to your home.

Woven Baskets

Traditional baskets handcrafted from local materials, showcasing the islands' rich weaving heritage.

Spicy and flavorful sauces that are a staple in Canarian cuisine, perfect for adding zest to your dishes at home.

Staying Safe

To do:

1: Have Fun

2: Stay Safe

Safe Routes

Stick to well-lit streets and avoid isolated areas after dark. The Canary Islands are generally safe, but it's prudent to exercise caution, especially in nightlife districts.

Water Safety

Swimming in designated areas, staying informed about weather conditions, and using safety equipment are essential for enjoying water activities safely in the Canary Islands.

Stay Informed and Prepared

  • Know Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency contacts, including local authorities and your country's embassy or consulate.
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a situation or area feels uncomfortable, trust your instincts and remove yourself from it. Prioritize your safety at all times.
