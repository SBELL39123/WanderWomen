FAQ

The travel guides are created by a woman who solo travels. Each city is one of the many cities she has visited. These are hostels, restaurants, tours, hidden gems, and overall tips that the Wander Women founder has wandered to.

Essential items include a reliable backpack, a first-aid kit, emergency contact/hotel address sheet, a towel, a eyemask for sleeping, a portable charger, and weather-appropriate clothing. Always tailor your packing list to the specific environment you'll be exploring.

Prioritize safety by informing someone of your itinerary, partying responsibly, walking with purpose, and being aware of your surroundings. Consider taking a self-defense course and carrying a personal safety device.

Destinations known for their safety and welcoming environments include Switzerland, Ireland, Canada, and Nordic countries like Finland and Iceland. It's advisable to research each location's current safety status and cultural norms before visiting.

Download apps like Whatsapp to stay in contact with family back home. Consider joining online forums or social media groups dedicated to solo female travelers.

Begin by researching destinations known for their safety and welcoming environments for solo female travelers. Start with shorter trips to build confidence, and consider joining organized tours to ease into solo travel. Engaging with online communities can also provide support and advice.

Solo travel can be safe when proper precautions are taken. Research your destination's safety profile, stay vigilant, and trust your instincts. Many women travel solo without incident, but it's important to remain aware of your surroundings and take necessary safety measures.

Keep valuables secure by using money belts or hidden pouches. Avoid displaying expensive items like jewelry or electronics. Consider using anti-theft backpacks and always be mindful of your belongings, especially in crowded areas.

Stay in hostels, join group tours, attend local events, and participate in activities like cooking classes or hiking groups. Engaging with fellow travelers and locals can enrich your experience and provide companionship. All it takes is "Hi, my name is ____"

Maintain regular communication with friends and family back home. Engage in activities that interest you, such as joining local classes or volunteering. Remember, it's normal to feel lonely at times, but embracing your independence can also be empowering.

Learn basic phrases in the local language and familiarize yourself with cultural norms and customs. Showing respect for local traditions can enhance your interactions and help avoid misunderstandings.

If you feel unsafe, seek immediate assistance from local authorities or trusted individuals. Trust your instincts and remove yourself from uncomfortable situations. Having emergency contacts and knowing the local emergency numbers can be crucial.

It's natural to experience moments of loneliness. Engaging in activities, joining group tours, and staying in communal accommodations can provide opportunities for social interaction.

Stay connected with loved ones through regular communication. Creating a routine, exploring new hobbies, and joining local events can also help alleviate homesickness.

Set a daily budget and track your expenses. Use travel-friendly banking options that offer low fees for international transactions. Having a mix of payment methods, including cash and cards, can be beneficial.

Stay vigilant, avoid risky situations, and trust your instincts. Inform someone of your itinerary, keep copies of important documents, and be cautious when sharing personal information with strangers
