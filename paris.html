PARIS Guide

Are you happy to be in Paris? Oui.

Top Tourist Spots

See that one tower

Eiffel Tower

An iconic wrought-iron lattice tower located on the Champ de Mars, it offers panoramic views of Paris from its observation decks. Built for the 1889 Exposition Universelle, it's a must-visit landmark.

Louvre Museum

The world's largest art museum and a historic monument in Paris, it houses thousands of works from civilizations across the globe, including the famous "Mona Lisa."

Notre-Dame Cathedral

​Notre-Dame Cathedral, Paris's iconic Gothic masterpiece, reopened in December 2024, inviting visitors to experience its renewed splendor.

Musée d'Orsay

Housed in a former railway station, this museum features an extensive collection of 19th and early 20th-century art, including works by Monet, Van Gogh, and Degas.

Arc de Triomphe

Commissioned by Napoleon after his victory at Austerlitz, this monumental arch honors those who fought for France. Located at the western end of the Champs-Élysées, it offers a panoramic view of the city from its summit.

Canal Saint-Martin

A picturesque canal lined with trendy boutiques and cafés, it's a perfect spot for a leisurely stroll or a boat ride, offering a glimpse into Paris's more laid-back side.

Hidden Gems

Explore Paris beyond its famous landmarks by visiting these lesser-known spots that showcase the city's rich history, culture, and unique charm.

Goutte d'Or

Located in the 18th arrondissement, Goutte d'Or, meaning 'Drop of Gold' in French, is affectionately known as 'Little Africa'. This vibrant neighborhood is celebrated for its rich African heritage, evident in its bustling markets, diverse shops, and cultural establishments. Visitors can immerse themselves in a tapestry of African cultures, cuisines, and traditions, making it a dynamic and enriching destination.

Place Josephine Baker

Situated in the 13th arrondissement, this square honors the legendary entertainer and civil rights activist Josephine Baker. The area features a modern floating swimming pool and offers a unique riverside experience.

Cafe de Flor

Located in the 6th arrondissement, Café de Flore is one of Paris's oldest and most iconic cafés. Renowned for its classic Art Deco interior, it has been a gathering place for influential writers and philosophers. Notably, American writer James Baldwin penned much of his first novel, 'Go Tell It on the Mountain', while frequenting this café, contributing to its rich literary heritage.

Printemps Haussman

A historic department store offering a wide range of luxury goods. Beyond shopping, visitors can enjoy panoramic views of Paris from its rooftop terrace.

Cultural Encounters

Paris, often dubbed the "City of Light," is a vibrant tapestry of art, fashion, cuisine, and traditions. The city seamlessly blends its rich history with modern influences, offering a unique cultural experience.

Art and Architecture

Paris's streets are akin to an open-air museum, with its historic architecture and numerous art galleries. The Musée d'Orsay, for instance, showcases Parisian poster art from the 1880s and 1890s, reflecting the city's artistic evolution.

Fashion Capital

Paris is renowned as a global fashion hub, hosting prestigious events like Paris Fashion Week. The city's boutiques and ateliers set trends that influence the world.

Culinary Delights

Parisian cuisine is a gastronomic journey, from Michelin-starred establishments to charming cafés. The city's diverse food scene reflects its multicultural heritage, with influences from various immigrant communities. The tradition of the "apéro" is a cherished pre-meal ritual, fostering social connections over drinks and light bites.

Everyday Life

Parisians value a balanced lifestyle, often enjoying long, leisurely meals and frequenting local markets. The city's numerous parks and gardens provide serene spots for relaxation amidst urban life. The "bise," a customary cheek kiss greeting, exemplifies the warmth and formality of French social interactions.

Festivals and Events

The city hosts a plethora of festivals, celebrating everything from music and film to food and fashion, reflecting its dynamic cultural scene. Events like Paris Fashion Week significantly influence both the fashion and culinary landscapes, with exclusive menus and soirées complementing the shows.

Gastronomic Delights

Paris's culinary scene is a dynamic blend of tradition and innovation, where classic dishes like escargots and croissants are reimagined in contemporary settings, reflecting the city's evolving gastronomic landscape. *All can be veganized or vegetarianized.*

To Eat

  • Soufflé:

    A light, fluffy dish made with a flavorful base, such as cheese or chocolate, and beaten egg whites, baked to a delicate rise.

  • Steak Frites:

    A classic Parisian meal consisting of a juicy steak paired with crispy, golden fries, often served with a variety of sauces.

  • Escargots de Bourgogne:

    A delicacy of tender snails baked in a rich garlic and parsley butter, offering a unique and savory taste experience.

  • Foie Gras:

    A luxurious pâté made from the liver of a duck or goose, celebrated for its rich, buttery flavor and smooth texture.

  • Crêpes:

    Thin, delicate pancakes that can be filled with a variety of sweet or savory fillings, making for a versatile meal or dessert.

  • French Onion Soup:

    A comforting soup made from caramelized onions in a rich broth, topped with melted cheese and toasted bread.

  • Boeuf Bourguignon:

    A hearty beef stew braised in red wine, often Burgundy, with onions, mushrooms, and bacon, embodying the essence of French country cooking.

  • Coq au Vin:

    A traditional French dish where chicken is slowly cooked in red wine, lardons, mushrooms, and garlic, resulting in a rich and flavorful meal.

  • Quiche Lorraine:

    A savory tart filled with a creamy custard of eggs, cream, and bacon, encased in a flaky pastry crust.

  • Madeleines:

    Small, shell-shaped sponge cakes with a distinctive hump, often flavored with lemon or almonds, and dusted with powdered sugar.

Where to Stay

Paris's 20 arrondissements, each with its own unique personality and charm, collectively contribute to the city's rich and diverse character.

Résidence de Bourgogne

Located at 15 Rue du Faubourg Saint-Martin in the 10th arrondissement, this residence offers comfortable accommodations with amenities like free Wi-Fi, a bar, and a restaurant. It's conveniently situated near the Strasbourg-Saint-Denis metro station, providing easy access to various parts of the city.

St. Christopher's Inn Paris Canal

Situated along the Canal Saint-Martin, this award-winning hostel features modern pod beds, free Wi-Fi, and a lively bar with regular events. It's just three metro stops from Gare du Nord.

The People Paris Nation

Located at Place de la Nation, this hostel offers stylish spaces inspired by the dynamic neighborhood. Guests can enjoy a variety of activities and a vibrant atmosphere.

Le Marais

Located in the 3rd and 4th arrondissements, Le Marais is known for its historic architecture, trendy boutiques, and vibrant art scene. It's a lively area with a mix of old and new, offering a unique Parisian experience.

Souvenir Ideas

Things to remember Paris by:

Parisian Scarves

A stylish scarf is a quintessential Parisian accessory. Available in various materials and designs, it's a fashionable and practical souvenir.

French Soaps

Luxurious soaps from renowned French brands like L'Occitane make for aromatic and useful keepsakes.

Parisian Art Prints

Affordable art prints from local galleries or flea markets capture the city's artistic flair and make for memorable souvenirs.

Vintage Postcards

Discover vintage postcards capturing Paris's timeless beauty, offering a nostalgic glimpse into the city's rich history.

Macarons

Indulge in Paris's iconic macarons, available in a variety of flavors and colors, perfect for satisfying your sweet tooth.

Staying Safe

To do:

1: Have Fun

2: Stay Safe

Beware of Pickpockets

Pickpocketing is common in crowded areas, especially near major tourist attractions. Keep your valuables secure and be cautious of distractions.

Be Aware of Common Scams

Scams such as the gold ring trick, friendship bracelet approach, and petition fraud are prevalent. Politely decline and walk away if approached.

Use Reputable Transportation Services

To avoid potential scams, use registered taxi services or ride-sharing apps, and agree on fares beforehand if meters are not available.

Avoid Displaying Valuables

Refrain from displaying expensive items like jewelry, cameras, or electronics that might attract unwanted attention.

Exercise Caution with Street Vendors

Be cautious when engaging with street vendors, as some may sell counterfeit goods or use aggressive tactics. Purchase from licensed vendors and stores to ensure authenticity.

Phone Safety

​To protect your phone from theft in Paris, keep it secure in a zipped bag or front pocket, avoid using it near metro doors, and remain vigilant in crowded areas.

Stay Informed and Prepared

  • Know Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency contacts, including local authorities and your country's embassy or consulate.
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a situation or area feels uncomfortable, trust your instincts and remove yourself from it. Prioritize your safety at all times.
