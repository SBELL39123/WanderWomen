Top Tourist Spots

Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal, is a city rich in history, culture, and spirituality

Boudhanath Stupa

One of the largest spherical stupas in Nepal, Boudhanath Stupa is a significant pilgrimage site for Buddhists. Its massive mandala makes it one of the largest in the world.

Durbar Square

Kathmandu Durbar Square is a historic palace complex that served as the royal palace for the Malla kings. It's a UNESCO World Heritage Site, showcasing traditional Newari architecture.

Garden of Dreams

An oasis in the heart of the city, the Garden of Dreams is a neoclassical historical garden featuring ponds, fountains, and a variety of flora, providing a peaceful retreat from the bustling streets.

Hanuman Dhoka Palace Complex

The historic palace complex was the royal residence during the Malla period. It features a series of courtyards, temples, and shrines, reflecting the architectural grandeur of the era.

Mount Everest and Nearby Attractions

While Kathmandu itself doesn't offer direct views of Mount Everest, nearby locations provide glimpses of this majestic peak. Additionally, Kathmandu serves as the gateway for various Everest-related adventures.

Hidden Gems

The cultural and artistic side of Buenos Aires. These lesser-known spots are full of character and provide a unique look into the city's vibrant neighborhoods, far from the usual tourist paths.

Swayambhunath Temple (Monkey Temple)

Known as the Monkey Temple, Swayambhunath offers panoramic views of Kathmandu Valley. The site features a stupa and numerous shrines, making it a significant Buddhist pilgrimage spot.

Bagwati Marg (Thamel District)

Bagwati Marg is a street located in the vibrant Thamel district of Kathmandu. Thamel is known for its bustling atmosphere, offering a wide range of shops, restaurants, hotels, and entertainment options. The area is popular among tourists for its lively nightlife and as a hub for cultural experiences.

Museum of Nepali Art

The Museum of Nepali Art showcases a diverse collection of traditional and contemporary Nepali art. The museum hosts various exhibitions, including art biennales, and plays a significant role in promoting Nepal's rich artistic heritage.

Cultural Encounters

The city harmoniously blends Hindu and Buddhist traditions, evident in its festivals, rituals, and daily life.

Religious Sites

Kathmandu is home to several significant religious sites, including the Pashupatinath Temple, a sacred Hindu temple complex, and the Swayambhunath Stupa (Monkey Temple), a prominent Buddhist pilgrimage site.

Festivals and Celebrations

The city celebrates numerous festivals with fervor, such as Dashain, Tihar, and Holi, reflecting its rich cultural heritage and communal harmony.

Arts and Crafts

Kathmandu's artisans excel in wood carving, metal casting, and weaving, producing intricate crafts that are both artistic and functional.

Living Traditions

Kathmandu's living traditions, such as the practice of Kumari (the living goddess) and the celebration of various street festivals (jatras), offer a unique insight into the city's cultural fabric.

Gastronomic Delights

Kathmandu's culinary scene is a vibrant fusion of flavors, influenced by its diverse ethnic communities and neighboring regions. From street vendors to upscale restaurants, the city offers a plethora of gastronomic experiences that cater to all palates. *All can be veganized or vegetarianized.*

To Eat

  • Dal Bhat: A traditional meal consisting of lentil soup (dal) served with steamed rice (bhat) and accompanied by vegetable curry (tarkari). It's a staple in Nepali households and offers a balanced and flavorful experience.
  • Momos: Nepal's beloved dumplings, filled with either minced meat or vegetables, and served with a spicy dipping sauce. They are a popular street food and a favorite snack among locals and tourists alike.
  • Sel Roti: A traditional homemade ring-shaped rice donut, deep-fried to a golden crisp. It's commonly prepared during festivals and special occasions, offering a sweet and crunchy treat.
  • Thali A Nepali Thali is a traditional meal comprising steamed rice, lentil soup (dal), vegetable curry (tarkari), meat curry (maasu), sautéed greens (saag), pickle (achar), and yogurt (dahi), offering a harmonious blend of flavors and textures.

Where to Stay

From budget-friendly hostels in Thamel to luxurious hotels in areas like Boudhanath and Patan, catering to travelers seeking both cultural immersion and modern comforts.

Hotel Serenity-Kathmandu

Located in the heart of Thamel, Hotel Serenity offers contemporary rooms with modern amenities, providing a comfortable stay for travelers exploring Kathmandu's vibrant streets.

Boudhanath Area

The Boudhanath area is renowned for its luxurious hotels and serene environment, making it an ideal choice for travelers seeking tranquility near the iconic Boudhanath Stupa.

Patan

Patan, also known as Lalitpur, boasts a range of boutique hotels that blend traditional Newari architecture with modern amenities, offering guests a unique cultural experience.

Souvenir Ideas

Things to remember Kathmandu by:

Pashmina Shawls

Soft, luxurious shawls made from the undercoat of Himalayan goats, available in various colors and patterns.

Thangka Paintings

Buenos Aires is famous for its high-quality leather products, including jackets, bags, wallets, and belts. These handcrafted items make for a timeless and stylish souvenir.

Singing Bowls

Tango is a cultural symbol of Buenos Aires, and you can bring home tango-themed souvenirs like dance shoes, clothing, or CDs of traditional tango music.

Handmade Lokta Paper Products

Eco-friendly products made from the bark of the Daphne plant, including notebooks, journals, and greeting cards, showcasing traditional Nepalese craftsmanship.

Tibetan Prayer Flags

Colorful flags printed with prayers, believed to spread goodwill and positive vibrations when fluttered by the wind.

Staying Safe

To do:

1: Have Fun

2: Stay Safe

Phone Safety

  • Keep Your Phone Secure: Petty theft is common in busy areas like tourist attractions and public transport. Avoid using your phone in crowded spaces and always keep it in a secure pocket or bag.
  • Use a Secure Phone Case: Consider using a case with a wrist strap or a pouch that makes it harder to snatch or access from the outside.

Water & Food Safety

  • Drink Bottled Water: Avoid tap water and always drink bottled water from trusted brands to avoid waterborne diseases.
  • Eat at Verified Restaurants: To avoid foodborne illnesses, eat at well-reviewed or recommended restaurants, and avoid street food unless it is cooked fresh in front of you.
  • Wash Your Hands: Make sure to wash your hands before eating and after using public transportation or visiting busy areas.

Blending In with Local Culture

  • Respect the Local Customs: Kathmandu has a rich cultural heritage. Be mindful of local traditions, such as removing your shoes when entering temples and shrines.
  • Dress Modestly: While Kathmandu is more relaxed than other parts of Nepal, dressing modestly, especially when visiting religious sites, is appreciated by the locals.
  • Be Cautious with Photography: Always ask for permission before photographing people, especially in religious contexts, as some locals may find it disrespectful.

Safe Hiking Gear

  • Wear Proper Hiking Boots: Make sure to wear sturdy, comfortable boots with good grip when hiking in the mountainous regions around Kathmandu.
  • Bring a First-Aid Kit: Always carry a small first-aid kit with essentials such as bandages, antiseptic, painkillers, and altitude sickness medication when hiking or trekking.
  • Use Verified Gear: For trekking in areas like Everest Base Camp, use verified gear from trusted shops or agencies, and ensure your guide is certified.

Stay Informed and Prepared

  • Know Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency contacts, including local authorities and your country's embassy or consulate.
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a situation or area feels uncomfortable, trust your instincts and remove yourself from it. Prioritize your safety at all times.
