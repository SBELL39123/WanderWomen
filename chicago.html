CHICAGO Guide

With so many things to do, you'll never grow bored

Top Tourist Spots

Become a local.

Cloud Gate

Also known as "The Bean," this iconic sculpture by Anish Kapoor is located in Millennium Park. It is famous for its reflective surface that distorts the city's skyline and provides visitors with a unique photographic opportunity.

Chicago Riverwalk

A scenic path along the Chicago River, the Riverwalk is lined with cafes, bars, and outdoor seating. It's a great spot for walking, dining, or taking a boat tour while enjoying views of the city's famous architecture.

Magnificent Mile

A famous stretch of Michigan Avenue, the Magnificent Mile is home to luxury shopping, high-end hotels, and historic landmarks. It's one of the busiest and most vibrant streets in Chicago, perfect for shopping, dining, and sightseeing.

Navy Pier

One of Chicago's most popular tourist attractions, Navy Pier features an amusement park, museums, restaurants, and boat tours. Visitors can enjoy rides like the Centennial Wheel and stunning views of Lake Michigan.

Millennium Park

This public park in the heart of Chicago is home to Cloud Gate, the Jay Pritzker Pavilion, and beautiful green spaces. It's a hub for outdoor concerts, art exhibits, and community events, making it a must-visit for anyone in the city.

North Avenue Beach

Located along the shores of Lake Michigan, North Avenue Beach is a popular spot for sunbathing, volleyball, and water sports during the summer. It also offers a beautiful view of the city skyline, making it a favorite among locals and visitors alike.

Hidden Gems

​Chicago's hidden gems offer unique experiences beyond the typical tourist attractions.

Rooftop Cinema Club

A unique outdoor cinema experience with stunning views of Chicago’s skyline. Watch classic and contemporary films while enjoying comfortable seating, food, and drinks on the rooftop.

Dorian's Speakeasy

A hidden gem in Chicago, Dorian’s Speakeasy is a 1920s-inspired bar offering craft cocktails, live music, and an intimate atmosphere. It’s a great spot for a sophisticated night out in the city.

Myopic Books

A cozy, independent bookstore located in Chicago’s Wicker Park. Myopic Books offers an extensive collection of used books, making it a favorite destination for book lovers and collectors.

Blind Barber
\

Blind Barber is a unique bar and barbershop hybrid located in Chicago, offering a craft cocktail menu and barber services. Enjoy a drink while getting a haircut or relaxing in this trendy and welcoming space.

Logan Square Farmer's Market

A vibrant community market located in the Logan Square neighborhood, offering a diverse selection of fresh produce, artisanal foods, and local crafts. Open year-round, it fosters a strong connection between consumers and local producers, making it a must-visit destination for food enthusiasts and community members alike.

Cultural Encounters

Chicago celebrates its rich cultural tapestry through numerous events and festivals that take place throughout the year. These gatherings showcase the city's diversity and community spirit.

Diversity in Chicago

  • Chinatown: A hub of Chinese culture, offering authentic cuisine, cultural festivals, and shops that reflect the rich heritage of its residents.
  • Pilsen: Known for its vibrant Mexican-American community, Pilsen features colorful murals, Mexican restaurants, and the National Museum of Mexican Art.
  • Greektown: A lively district offering Greek cuisine, cultural events, and the National Hellenic Museum, celebrating the contributions of Greek-Americans.

Chicago Festivals

  • Lollapalooza: An annual four-day music festival held in Grant Park, featuring a diverse lineup of artists across multiple genres.
  • St. Patrick's Day Parade: A vibrant procession celebrating Irish culture, with floats, bagpipers, and the tradition of dyeing the Chicago River green.
  • Mexican Independence Day Festival: A celebration in September featuring parades, music, dance, and authentic Mexican cuisine to commemorate Mexico's independence.
  • Chicago Air and Water Show: A spectacular August event showcasing aerial acrobatics and water-based performances along Lake Michigan.
  • Chicago Blues Festival: A free June festival in Millennium Park honoring the city's blues legacy with performances from renowned artists.
  • Chicago Jazz Festival: Held over Labor Day weekend, this festival features performances from local and international jazz musicians.
  • Chicago Gourmet: A three-day event in September celebrating the city's culinary scene with tastings, demonstrations, and seminars.
  • Chicago House Music Festival and Conference: An annual event celebrating Chicago's iconic dance music genre with DJ sets and panel discussions.
  • Ribfest Chicago: A June festival offering a variety of rib vendors and live music in the Lincoln Square neighborhood.
  • Andersonville Midsommarfest: A June celebration of Swedish heritage with music, food, and art in the Andersonville neighborhood.

Music Scene

Chicago's music scene is deeply rooted in genres like blues, jazz, gospel, and hip-hop. House music originates in Chicago's underground club scene in the early 1980s. Iconic venues such as Buddy Guy's Legends and the House of Blues continue to celebrate this rich musical heritage. The city also hosts numerous music festivals, including the Chicago Blues Festival and Lollapalooza, attracting artists and fans from around the world.

Theater and Performing Arts

Chicago boasts a vibrant theater scene, with institutions like the Goodman Theatre and Steppenwolf Theatre Company producing both classic and contemporary works. The city also hosts the Chicago Performs festival, featuring groundbreaking performances by local artists.

Public Art Installations

The cityscape of Chicago is adorned with public art, including sculptures, murals, and interactive installations. Notable works like the "Chicago Picasso" in Daley Plaza and the "Flamingo" by Alexander Calder contribute to the city's vibrant cultural identity.

Gastronomic Delights

Chicago's culinary scene is a tapestry of global flavors, offering everything from deep-dish pizza and Chicago-style hot dogs to international cuisines. The city's diverse food offerings are a testament to its multicultural population, with neighborhoods like Pilsen and Devon Avenue showcasing Mexican and Indian culinary traditions, respectively. *All can be veganized or vegetarianized.*

To Eat

  • Deep-Dish Pizza: A Chicago staple, deep-dish pizza features a thick, buttery crust filled with layers of mozzarella cheese, savory tomato sauce, and various toppings. Baked in a deep, oiled pan, this pizza offers a rich and hearty flavor. Notable pizzerias like Lou Malnati's and Pizzeria Uno are famed for their exceptional deep-dish offerings.
  • Chicago-Style Hot Dog: This iconic hot dog features a Vienna Beef frankfurter nestled in a steamed poppy seed bun, topped with mustard, chopped onions, sweet pickle relish, tomato slices, sport peppers, a dill pickle spear, and a sprinkle of celery salt. Purists insist on avoiding ketchup, embracing the "drag it through the garden" philosophy of toppings.
  • Italian Beef Sandwich: Thinly sliced seasoned beef, slow-cooked in its own juices, served on a crusty Italian roll. Options include adding sweet peppers or spicy giardiniera, and for an extra indulgence, a slice of Italian sausage can be added to create a "Combo."
  • Chicago Mix Popcorn: A delectable blend of caramel and cheese popcorn, creating a sweet and savory snack experience. Garrett Popcorn Shops popularized this mix, offering it in signature blue-striped tins that are a familiar sight to Chicagoans and visitors alike.

Where to Stay

Chicago boasts a variety of neighborhoods, each offering unique experiences for visitors. Here are some top areas to consider for your stay:

The Loop

As Chicago's central business district, The Loop offers proximity to major attractions like Millennium Park and the Art Institute of Chicago. Staying here provides easy access to public transportation and a variety of dining options.

River North

Known for its vibrant art scene, River North is home to numerous galleries, upscale restaurants, and lively nightlife. Its central location ensures quick access to other parts of the city.

Lakeview

Situated along Lake Michigan, Lakeview offers a mix of residential charm and entertainment. It's known for Wrigley Field and a variety of dining and shopping options.

Souvenir Ideas

Things to remember Chicago by:

Chicago Deep-Dish Pizza Pan

Bring home the essence of Chicago's iconic deep-dish pizza with a high-quality pizza pan. Many kitchenware stores in Chicago offer these pans, allowing you to recreate this culinary delight at home.

Chicago Sports Team Merchandise

Chicago is home to several beloved sports teams. Official merchandise such as jerseys, hats, and other memorabilia from the Chicago Bears (NFL), Chicago Bulls (NBA), Chicago Cubs (MLB), and Chicago Blackhawks (NHL) make for great souvenirs.

Chicago Architecture Books

Chicago's architectural heritage is world-renowned. Books detailing the city's iconic buildings and architectural history are insightful keepsakes. These can be found in local bookstores and museum shops.

Chicago Blues Records

Chicago is the birthplace of electric blues. Vinyl records or CDs of classic Chicago blues artists like Muddy Waters and Howlin' Wolf are authentic souvenirs. Local record stores and music shops offer a wide selection.

Staying Safe

To do:

1: Have Fun

2: Stay Safe

Stay Aware of Your Surroundings

Remain vigilant and attentive to your environment. Avoid distractions such as excessive phone use or wearing headphones, especially in crowded or unfamiliar areas.

Keep Personal Belongings Secure

Keep wallets, phones, and other valuables in secure, concealed locations. Consider using anti-theft bags or money belts.

Avoid High-Risk Areas

Be cautious in neighborhoods known for higher crime rates, especially after dark. It's advisable to research and identify areas to avoid before your visit.

Report Suspicious Activity

If you observe suspicious behavior or feel unsafe, contact local authorities immediately. Trust your instincts and seek assistance when needed.

Stay Informed and Prepared

  • Know Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency contacts, including local authorities and your country's embassy or consulate.
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a situation or area feels uncomfortable, trust your instincts and remove yourself from it. Prioritize your safety at all times.
