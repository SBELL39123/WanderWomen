BARCELONA Guide

Should you move here? Yes.

Top Tourist Spots

Discover Barcelona’s iconic landmarks and hidden gems, from historic churches to beautiful beaches. Whether you’re an art lover, a history enthusiast, or a beachgoer, Barcelona offers something for every traveler.

La Sagrada Familia

This stunning basilica designed by Antoni Gaudí is an architectural masterpiece, with its towering spires and intricate facades. It’s an iconic symbol of Barcelona’s rich cultural heritage.

La Rambla

Barcelona’s most famous street, La Rambla is a bustling promenade lined with shops, restaurants, and street performers. It’s the perfect spot for a leisurely stroll, offering a vibrant mix of culture and entertainment.

Barceloneta

Located near the Barceloneta neighborhood, this popular beach is known for its golden sands and lively atmosphere. It's ideal for swimming, sunbathing, and enjoying delicious seafood at the nearby restaurants.

Parc Güell

A UNESCO World Heritage site, Park Güell is another of Gaudí’s creations, featuring whimsical architecture and lush gardens. Visitors can explore its colorful mosaics, sweeping views of the city, and the famous lizard sculpture, “El Drac.”

Camp Nou

Home to FC Barcelona, Camp Nou is the largest football stadium in Europe. It’s an iconic venue for sports fans and visitors can tour the stadium to explore its history, locker rooms, and trophy collection.

Hidden Gems

Discover Barcelona's vibrant cultural scene in neighborhoods like Gràcia and El Born, where colorful murals, historic sites, and unique shops tell the city's rich and artistic story. These hidden gems offer a more intimate experience of the city's cultural side, far from the tourist crowds.

Tot 1 Euro Bar

Located at Carrer d'Aribau 15, this lively bar offers a unique concept where everything, from drinks to snacks, is priced at just 1 euro.

Els Encants Market

Situated in the L'Eixample district, this historic market features a vast array of stalls selling everything from antiques to clothing, all under a striking mirrored canopy.

Gràcia Neighborhoods

Known for its narrow winding streets, colonial-style houses, and vibrant street art, Gràcia offers a glimpse of Barcelona's authentic cultural scene, filled with art galleries, cozy bars, and unique shops.

Montserrat

Located about 30 kilometers northwest of Barcelona, is a multi-peaked mountain range famed for its unique rock formations and the Benedictine Monastery housing the revered Black Madonna. ​

Cultural Encounters

Immerse yourself in Barcelona's rich cultural heritage by visiting its numerous museums, historical sites, and vibrant festivals.

Music

Barcelona's music and dance scene is a vibrant fusion of genres and styles, from the passionate rhythms of rumba catalana pioneered by Antonio González 'El Pescaílla' to the contemporary sounds of artists like Rosalía and Manel, complemented by traditional dances such as flamenco, sardana, and hip-hop, reflecting the city's rich cultural tapestry.

Events

  • La Mercè: An annual festival held in September, featuring vibrant parades, traditional Catalan music, and street parties that fill the city with energy and colour.
  • Primavera Sound: A renowned music festival celebrated in June, attracting international artists and music lovers from around the globe.
  • Calçot Season: ​Barcelona's calçot season, from January to April, is a must-try culinary experience, with traditional calçotadas and innovative dishes like calçot-filled taiyaki delighting food enthusiasts. ​

Sports

Barcelona's sports culture is vibrant, with football being the most popular. The city is home to FC Barcelona, whose matches at Camp Nou are a must-see for sports enthusiasts.

Relaxed Lifestyle and Nightlife

Barcelona offers a laid-back lifestyle, with locals enjoying leisurely meals and late-night socializing. At night, the city comes alive with numerous clubs and bars, particularly in areas like El Raval and the Port Olímpic, offering diverse music genres and vibrant atmospheres.

Gastronomic Delights

Delight in Barcelona's rich culinary scene, where traditional Catalan dishes and international fusion cuisine come together to offer a variety of flavors. *All can be veganized or vegetarianized*

To Eat

  • Paella: A traditional Spanish rice dish originating from Valencia, paella combines saffron-infused rice with a variety of ingredients such as seafood, chicken, rabbit, and vegetables. It's renowned for its rich flavors and vibrant colors.
  • Tinto de Verano: A refreshing Spanish cocktail made by mixing red wine with lemon soda or gaseosa, served over ice. It's a popular beverage, especially during the summer months.
  • Churros: Fried dough pastries, typically sprinkled with sugar or cinnamon, and sometimes filled with chocolate, dulce de leche, or cream. They are a popular breakfast treat or snack in many Spanish-speaking countries.
  • Pan amb tomàquet (Pan con Tomate): A classic Catalan tapa consisting of toasted bread rubbed with fresh garlic, topped with grated ripe tomatoes, drizzled with olive oil, and sprinkled with salt. It's a simple yet flavorful dish.
  • Patatas Bravas: A traditional Spanish tapa featuring fried potato cubes topped with a spicy tomato sauce known as 'salsa brava'. The sauce typically includes ingredients like tomato paste, olive oil, paprika, and sometimes garlic.

Where to Stay

Immerse yourself in Barcelona's dynamic hospitality landscape, where each neighborhood presents a distinctive fusion of character, heritage, and excitement.

Kabul Party Hostel

Located in the heart of Barcelona's vibrant Plaça Reial, Kabul Party Hostel is renowned for its lively atmosphere and social environment. As one of Europe's premier party hostels, it offers guests an unforgettable experience with nightly events and a central location just steps away from Las Ramblas and the Gothic Quarter.

Gràcia Neighborhood

Situated just a short walk from the iconic Passeig de Gràcia, the Gràcia neighborhood is known for its bohemian vibe, lively squares, and a plethora of bars and restaurants. It's an ideal area for travelers seeking a vibrant atmosphere and proximity to cultural landmarks.

El Born Neighborhood

El Born is a trendy district known for its narrow streets, boutique shops, and vibrant nightlife. It's also home to cultural attractions like the Picasso Museum and the beautiful Santa Maria del Mar church. Staying in this area offers a blend of historical charm and modern amenities.

L'Eixample Neighborhood

Renowned for its modernist architecture and wide, grid-like streets, L'Eixample is a central district that offers a mix of shopping, dining, and cultural experiences. It's also home to iconic landmarks such as the Sagrada Familia and Casa Batlló.

Souvenir Ideas

Things to remember Barcelona by:

Ceramic Tiles (Azulejos)

Hand-painted tiles featuring colorful mosaics and traditional Catalan designs, perfect for adding a touch of Barcelona's artistic heritage to your home.

Espadrilles (Espardenyes)

Traditional Catalan footwear made from canvas or esparto rope, known for their comfort and timeless style.

Cava

A bottle of this renowned sparkling wine from the Penedès region encapsulates the celebratory spirit of Catalonia.

Hand-Fans

Delicately crafted fans that are both practical and stylish, reflecting the city's flair for design.

Panot Tiles

Inspired by Gaudí's mosaics, these tiles serve as a colorful reminder of Barcelona's architectural genius.

Staying Safe

To do:

1: Have Fun

2: Stay Safe

Secure Your Belongings

  • Use Anti-Theft Accessories: Opt for cross-body bags or money belts with secure closures to keep your valuables safe. Anti-theft backpacks are also highly recommended.
  • Avoid Displaying Valuables: Keep expensive items like jewelry, cameras, and large amounts of cash out of sight to minimize attracting unwanted attention.

Popular Tourist Scams

  • Be Cautious in Tourist Hotspots: Be extra aware of your surroundings in crowded places such as public transportation, markets, and popular tourist sites, as these are common spots for pickpockets.
  • Be Aware of Common Scams: Watch out for distractions or approaches that may be tactics used by thieves. Always keep an eye on your possessions.

Stay Informed and Prepared

  • Know Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency contacts, including local authorities and your country's embassy or consulate.
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a situation or area feels uncomfortable, trust your instincts and remove yourself from it. Prioritize your safety at all times.
