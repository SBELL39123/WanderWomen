ISTANBUL Guide

The city itself is a Turkish Delight

Top Tourist Spots

Discover Istanbul's rich history and vibrant culture through its iconic landmarks.

Grand Bazaar

One of the world's largest and oldest covered markets, offering a labyrinth of shops selling everything from spices to jewelry. It's a shopper's paradise and a cultural experience.

The Blue Mosque

Officially known as the Sultan Ahmed Mosque, this 17th-century architectural marvel is renowned for its six minarets and stunning blue Iznik tiles adorning its interior.

Hagia Sofia Grand Mosque

A masterpiece of Byzantine architecture, Hagia Sophia has served as a church, mosque, and museum, reflecting Istanbul's diverse cultural heritage.

Basilica Cistern

An ancient underground water reservoir featuring a forest of columns and a serene atmosphere, offering a unique glimpse into the city's engineering prowess.

Topkapi Palace Museum

The former palace of Ottoman sultans, now a museum showcasing imperial collections, including the famous Topkapi Dagger and the Spoonmaker's Diamond.

Sultanahmet Square

The heart of Istanbul's historic district, surrounded by major landmarks and offering a lively atmosphere with cafes and shops. It's a great starting point for exploring the city's rich history.

Hidden Gems

Not-so touristy places on the European and Asian side.

Anadolu Kavağı

A picturesque fishing village on Istanbul's Asian shore, offering fresh seafood restaurants and panoramic views of the Bosphorus from its hilltop castle.

Hammam

Immerse yourself in Istanbul's rich bathing culture by visiting these historic hammams, each offering a unique and rejuvenating experience.

Çamlica Hill

The highest point in Istanbul, offering panoramic views of the city and the Bosphorus, surrounded by a large public park perfect for picnics and relaxation.

Obelisk of Theodosius

An ancient Egyptian obelisk located in Sultanahmet Square, dating back to the 4th century AD, showcasing intricate carvings and historical inscriptions.

Pudding Shop Lale: Hippie Trail

​The Pudding Shop, officially Lale Restaurant, became a central meeting point for travelers on the 1960s and 1970s Hippie Trail, offering traditional Turkish desserts like Tavuk Göğsü.

Cultural Encounters

Istanbul, a city where East meets West, boasts a rich tapestry of cultures, blending Byzantine and Ottoman influences with vibrant contemporary arts, fashion, and culinary scenes.

Traditional Crafts

Explore Istanbul's rich tradition of craftsmanship, including ceramics, mosaics, and the art of ceramic evil eye amulets. Engage in hands-on workshops to create your own piece of this cultural heritage.

Religious Diversity

Istanbul's rich history as a former capital of both the Byzantine and Ottoman empires has fostered a diverse religious landscape. The city is predominantly Muslim, with numerous mosques such as the iconic Blue Mosque gracing its skyline. However, Istanbul also hosts communities practicing Christianity and Judaism, contributing to its multicultural identity. This blend of religious traditions is evident in the city's festivals, rituals, and daily life, offering visitors a glimpse into its complex spiritual heritage.

Culinary Delights

Istanbul's culinary scene is a fusion of Middle Eastern, Mediterranean, and Balkan flavors. From street food like simit and dürüm to fine dining, the city offers a gastronomic journey for every palate.

Cultural Practices

Traditional practices such as communal bathing in hammams play a significant role in Istanbul's social life. These baths, like the historic Zeyrek Çinili Hamam, offer not only physical cleansing but also foster community bonds, providing emotional benefits and a sense of belonging.

Gastronomic Delights

A harmonious fusion of diverse cultures, with rich Ottoman heritage meeting modern tastes, creating a vibrant food scene that spans from street food stalls to fine dining. *All can be veganized or vegetarianized.*

To Eat

  • Kebabs:

    Grilled meats, often lamb or chicken, served with rice or flatbread and a side of vegetables, are a staple of Turkish cuisine, showcasing the bold flavors of the region.

  • Meze:

    A selection of small dishes that can include dips, salads, and cold appetizers, offering a variety of flavors such as hummus, baba ganoush, and tzatziki.

  • Baklava:

    A sweet pastry made of layers of filo dough filled with ground nuts and sweetened with syrup or honey, perfect for those with a sweet tooth.

  • Turkish Delight (Lokums):

    A soft, chewy confectionery flavored with rosewater, lemon, or pomegranate, often dusted with powdered sugar, a perfect treat to enjoy with Turkish coffee.

  • Roasted Chestnuts (Kestane):

    A popular street snack, especially during the cooler months, where chestnuts are roasted over hot coals, offering a warm and nutty flavor. Vendors can be found throughout Istanbul, particularly in busy areas like Istiklal Street. [Discover more about Istanbul's roasted chestnuts](https://thegaboronerunner.com/2024/06/25/three-iconic-street-foods-in-istanbul-gluten-free-diaries/)

  • Dolma:

    Vegetables such as grape leaves, peppers, or zucchinis stuffed with a mixture of rice, pine nuts, and herbs, offering a delightful blend of flavors.

  • Hummus:

    A creamy blend of mashed chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, and garlic, commonly served as a dip or spread, reflecting the Middle Eastern influence on Turkish cuisine.

Where to Stay

​Istanbul's neighborhoods, each with its own unique character and charm, collectively contribute to the city's rich and diverse identity.

Bahaus Hostel Otel

Located in Sultanahmet, Bahaus Guesthouse Hostel offers comfortable dormitories and private rooms, a lively rooftop terrace with panoramic city views, and is within walking distance of major attractions like the Hagia Sophia and Blue Mosque.

Beyoğlu

Beyoğlu is renowned for its vibrant nightlife, diverse dining options, and bustling shopping streets. The area around Taksim Square and Istiklal Avenue is especially lively, offering numerous bars, clubs, and entertainment venues. It's also home to cultural sites like the historic Galata Tower.

Kadıköy

Situated on the Asian side of Istanbul, Kadıköy is known for its youthful atmosphere, lively markets, and a wide array of cafes and bars. The area offers a more laid-back vibe compared to the European side, with a thriving arts scene and a variety of music venues.

Fener and Balat

These neighboring districts offer a glimpse into Istanbul's rich history, with colorful houses, antique shops, and a growing number of cafes and art spaces. The area's unique blend of cultures and histories provides a fascinating backdrop for exploration.

Souvenir Ideas

Things to remember Istanbul by:

Turkish Towels (Pestemal)

Lightweight and absorbent, these cotton towels come in various colors and patterns, making them both practical and stylish souvenirs.

Turkish Coffee Sets

Elegant porcelain or copper sets, including a cezve (coffee pot) and demitasse cups, perfect for recreating the authentic Turkish coffee experience at home.

Blue Evil Eye (Nazar Boncuğu)

A traditional amulet believed to protect against negative energy, available in various forms like keychains, jewelry, and wall hangings.

Iznik Ceramics

Hand-painted pottery featuring intricate floral designs and vibrant colors, reflecting centuries of Turkish craftsmanship.

Staying Safe

To do:

Respecting Local Culture

While Istanbul is cosmopolitan, dressing modestly, especially when visiting religious sites, is appreciated. Women should cover their shoulders and knees, and carry a scarf to cover their head when entering mosques.

Be Aware of Common Scams

Be cautious of scams targeting tourists, such as the gold ring trick or aggressive street vendors. Politely decline offers that seem suspicious.

Be Cautious with Alcohol Consumption

Be wary of consuming alcohol from unlicensed sources. There have been incidents of counterfeit alcohol leading to serious health issues and fatalities. Stick to reputable establishments for alcoholic beverages.

Stay Informed and Prepared

  • Know Emergency Contacts: Keep a list of emergency contacts, including local authorities and your country's embassy or consulate.
  • Trust Your Instincts: If a situation or area feels uncomfortable, trust your instincts and remove yourself from it. Prioritize your safety at all times.
